# Carolina & Andres — coaching session (2026-05-12)

## Context

Carolina is a Buda HIVE Cohort 4 participant exploring whether to launch a coworking / community space for women on her downtown Buda property (220 Austin Street, ~1,200 sqft house + ~7,500 sqft backyard). Coming into the session she'd surveyed ~6 women and was leaning toward dropping the idea because the artisan-entrepreneur respondents (candle-maker, macramé maker) said they needed workshop space rather than a coworking desk, and another respondent said she gets free meeting space at the library. Andres pushed back: she'd surveyed the wrong ideal client persona.

## Key decisions

1. **Don't kill the women-coworking idea.** The survey data was disqualifying because Carolina interviewed the wrong personas (artisan makers who need workshop space, not desk space; and frugal solopreneurs who use the library for free). Reframe the ICP.
2. **Reframe ICP** as women who already have income (paycheck or revenue) and want a community + a place to get out of the house — not entrepreneurs struggling to find free workspace. Buda demographics support this: median household income ~$120K, 50.1% bachelor's degree attainment, 72% female labor-force participation, 49% female residents.
3. **Lead with "community + work," not wellness.** Women feel guilty buying wellness for themselves but will invest in business/work. Frame the spa, sauna, cold plunge, nails, etc. as "while you're already here working" perks, not the headline.
4. **The 7,500 sqft backyard is the social engine.** Interior (~1,200 sqft) becomes curated high-value: lounge (~350 sqft), 3 phone booths carved out of existing closets, coffee bar in a repurposed closet, one meeting room (~120 sqft), reception/check-in scanner.
5. **Convert the larger bedroom's bathroom into a cold plunge + sauna combo** (Costco-style zip sauna fits). The hallway bathroom stays as the shared restroom — avoids the "people staring at me when I exit" problem of having the shared bathroom in a room full of coworkers.
6. **Pricing direction:** $50–$100/month membership. At $100 × 25 members, the model already covers itself; 25–50 more on top → profitable.
7. **Sell corporate / institutional memberships, not just individual ones.** Examples Andres floated:
   - Buda EDC: $100/mo membership so Jackie + Casey can come work from the space and stay in front of women entrepreneurs.
   - Subaru (or similar): $300/mo for 3 seats — gets the dealership in front of affluent women buyers, gets Carolina a steady anchor revenue.
8. **Use Moil to drive ICP research.** Carolina signed up live during the meeting, got 30-day free trial activated. (Andres also disclosed in confidence that Buda EDC will give HIVE participants a 1-year Moil license — don't tell anyone yet.)
9. **Register a new business entity** for the coworking concept, separate from the real-estate business.

## Notable themes / coaching points (for KB synthesis)

- **"Plan your questions before asking them."** A coworking survey that just asks "would you pay for a coworking space?" is unfair to the idea. The right question depends on which ICP you're testing.
- **"Mary makes sense because she wants to save money — she's not your client unless she sees community as the benefit."** Distinguishing between feedback quality and feedback fit.
- **"Move fast, create fast, fail fast — but play it against your market research first."** Use Moil to pre-test ideas before spending money.
- **"How do I start tomorrow?"** vs. building a 10-year plan and never starting.
- **Karpathy-style "10-star product" thinking** showed up implicitly: don't downscale ambition based on the wrong survey; expand the offering (coworking + spa + events + corporate memberships) when the unit economics support it.
