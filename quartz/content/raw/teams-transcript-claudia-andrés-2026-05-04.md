## Decisions & action items

### Decisions (coaching guidance Andres gave Claudia)
- **Anchor everything on fixed costs first.** Until Claudia knows her exact monthly fixed costs, she can't size the marketing target. Floor: ~$2,800/mo to clear from Nativa.
- **No more "free" anything that doesn't trigger a purchase.** Replace giveaways with discounted bundles (e.g., free 1st microneedling → 2nd & 3rd at a steep discount; free Botox 50u → upsell full-face). Always an exchange, never a gift.
- **Differentiation is medical credentials**, not language or community. Build all education content around "this is medicine, not haircut + Botox." That's the moat against estheticians.
- **Hold off on the Buda Chamber of Commerce membership** for now — too late for May ribbon-cutting use, $400/yr. Hispanic Chamber ribbon-cutting is already booked for the May 21 event.
- **Don't chase Latino-cheapest-shopper segment.** Target ideal client persona: Buda households 35–54 with $120K+ income (Ruby Ranch, Elliot Ranch, +6 other neighborhoods on the doc Andres sent). Realtors and women business owners are the sweet spot — not teachers (no disposable income).
- **May 21 grand-opening event strategy:** focus on networking + sign-up sheet, not personalized consultations. Cap consults at "first 15 people who show on time." Lower the 70-person sign-up cap if it gets close to capacity.
