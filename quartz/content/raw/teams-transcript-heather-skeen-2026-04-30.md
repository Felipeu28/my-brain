## Key decisions

1. **MVP wedge = daycare CEU/workshop programs** — Heather will start there, not behavior analysts (overcrowded, requires social media presence) and not "everyone" (unfocused).
2. **Pricing structure shift** — move from one-off $250 hourly to **annual programs**. Example: 12 required PD topics split across 4 sessions = ~$1,000/year per daycare. Always ask client's PD budget before quoting.
3. **Holding company structure** — keep the therapy practice and the consulting/training arm in separate legal entities to insulate the clinical business from training-side liability.
4. **Name change → "Providence Counseling and Therapy"** (working candidate). Drop "Behavior" because it gates discovery (Shannon at Monday's HIVE meeting confirmed it: she dismissed Providence because the name read as behavior-only).
5. **Use lived experience as differentiator** — the Providence Hospital story (NICU, son born 35 weeks, name meaning = "timely preparation for future eventualities") is powerful and authentic; weave into website/intro materials.
