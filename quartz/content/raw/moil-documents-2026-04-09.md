## Marketig & Customer Acquisition

Moil will utilize a multi-faceted approach to reach its target customers of small to medium-sized businesses (SMBs) across the United States. Our go-to-market strategy integrates product-led growth, content marketing, strategic partnerships, and direct sales to maximize both reach and retention.

Funnel Approach

1. Awareness:

Content Marketing & SEO: Through blogs featuring case studies, market trends, and success stories, potential customers can see the value of Moil.

Social Media Campaigns: Target high-intent keywords to attract organic traffic via platforms like Google and social media.

Strategic Partnerships: Collaborate with organizations such as Buffer and Hootsuite for co-marketing opportunities and integration, enhancing our visibility.

2. Consideration:

Freemium Model: Potential clients can start with a free tier to experience our service without any financial risk.

Value Demonstration: Offering market research, business plans, and tailored content strategies can help prospects visualize their return on investment before they commit financially.

3. Conversion:

Onboarding: Simplified, no-credit-card-required onboarding ensures that users can start utilizing Moil’s capabilities immediately.

30-Day Money-Back Guarantee: This will reduce the perceived risk for potential subscribers when deciding to purchase.

4. Retention and Expansion:

Customer Success: Investing in a dedicated customer success team will help mitigate churn and ensure customers utilize all features effectively. Feedback will be continuously gathered to improve the platform and respond to user needs.

Upselling: Once users experience value, we can guide them towards higher tier plans with more comprehensive features, thereby increasing average revenue per user (ARPU).


## Acquisition Model Estimates

Monthly Marketing Budget: Estimated at $5,000, based on current operational costs.

Expected Reach / Impressions: According to industry benchmarks for small business SaaS, social media ads typically achieve around 1,000 impressions for every $100 spent. Thus, our budget might generate about 50,000 impressions per month.

Estimated Conversion Rates: Benchmarks suggest conversion rates from impressions to signups are typically 2-5% for SaaS businesses. Assuming a conservative 2%, this would translate to approximately 1,000 new visitors. Therefore, we'd expect about 20 new paying customers each month when assessed over time.

CAC Calculation: CAC = Monthly Marketing Spend / New Customers

Estimated CAC = $5,000 / 20 new customers = $250 per customer.

Payback Period: Assuming an ARPU of $200 (from a blend of subscription tiers):

Payback Period = CAC / (ARPU × Gross Margin)

Assuming a gross margin of 75%, Payback Period = $250 / ($200 × 0.75) = 1.67 months.

This acquisition model, alongside a strong retention strategy, positions Moil well for sustainable growth as we capitalize on the increasing trend of SMBs seeking integrated business solutions.
