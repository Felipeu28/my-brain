## Decisions & action items

### Decisions
- **Anchor the entire business plan on the Train Depot opportunity** (≈300–400 sq ft + outdoor patio). Below-average rent, 1-year initial lease, EDC-backed marketing tailwind, low downside (EDC will release them if it fails). This is the lowest-risk path to learning if the concept works.
- **Skip the Roundabout location.** Build-out loan + multi-year commitment kills the unit economics. Sarah (the bookstore-with-wine concept) was talked out of it for the same reason — confirmed by Andres.
- **De-emphasize kid-friendly positioning during the prime hours.** Each kid in the room ≈ 1 fewer paying adult; target date-night / professional clientele. Reserve kid-friendly programming for slow days only.
- **Treat Water To Wine and Sarah as direct competitors**, despite the friendly framing. Anyone buying wine from Kat & Christine is a buyer not buying from Dane (Water To Wine) or Sarah.
- **Differentiation pillars:** experience-driven small space + community/family-named custom-label wines (using Water To Wine's labeling service so they don't need to make wine themselves yet) + a Buda wine-tour book (printed or QR code).
- **Lock the 5-year dream; ship the 6-month version.** Don't chase the perfect forever-space.
- **Decide rent budget before any business-loan conversation.** A loan adds permanent overhead from day one and shouldn't be the first lever.
