Presentation Guide &amp; Talking Points: Unlocking Growth: AI for Small Business
Buda 2025 Entrepreneur Camp | June 18, 2025
Panelists: Andres Urrego (MoilApp) &amp; Joshua Edmond (JUCO AI)
Objective: Empower SMBs with actionable AI knowledge, address their struggles (e.g., limited resources, time, and tech expertise), and demonstrate practical tools like prompt engineering, a live landing page demo, and your free business plan creator and hiring platform. Ensure attendees leave inspired, confident, and equipped to implement AI in their businesses, with a clear message that in 2025, no question should go unanswered, and the sky is the limit for AI adoption.

Goal: Set the stage, establish credibility, connect with the audience’s challenges, and emphasize the urgency of embracing AI in 2025.
Talking Points:
Welcome and Context: "Good morning, everyone! I’m Andres Urrego, founder of MoilApp, where we’re closing the gap for offline workers and SMBs. Josh is the founder of JUCO AI, an academic success platform for community college students."

Today, we’re here to show you how AI can be your superpower, helping you save time, cut costs, and scale smarter."
Urgency of AI in 2025: "In 2025, no question should ever go unanswered when it comes to your business. Tools like Grok and ChatGPT make knowledge instantly accessible. Let’s not repeat the mistake we made with Google—getting complacent and missing its full potential. With AI, the sky is the limit for learning and implementation. It’s time to seize this opportunity and transform your business."
Set Expectations: "By the end of this session, you’ll understand what AI is, how it solves real SMB pain points, and walk away with practical steps to start using it. Plus, we’ll do a live 15-minute demo to create a landing page with one prompt and share two free tools from MoilApp to supercharge your growth."
Engagement Tip: Ask, “Raise your hand if you’ve ever felt overwhelmed by repetitive tasks or struggled to compete with bigger businesses.” Use this to segue into AI’s value.
2. What is Artificial Intelligence (AI)? (10 minutes, including Prompt Engineering)
Goal: Demystify AI, make it relatable, and teach prompt engineering as a core skill.
Talking Points:
AI Defined (2 min): "AI is technology that mimics human tasks—think learning from data to automate, predict, or optimize. It’s not sci-fi; it’s in tools you already use, like Google Maps, which optimizes routes by 15-20%. For SMBs, AI means working smarter, not harder."
Two Main Types (2 min):
Generative AI: "Tools like ChatGPT or Grok create content—blog posts, emails, even website designs."
Predictive AI: "Forecasts trends, like predicting inventory needs or customer behavior."
Why It Matters for SMBs (2 min): "AI levels the playing field. You don’t need a big team or budget to use it. It’s about solving problems like answering customer queries at 2 a.m. or targeting the right audience with less guesswork."
Prompt Engineering 101 (4 min):
Why Prompting Matters: "The secret to getting great results from AI, like ChatGPT or Grok, is asking the right questions. A good prompt is like giving clear instructions to an employee."
Structure of a Great Prompt:
Role: Tell the AI who it is (e.g., “Act as a marketing expert”).
Task: Specify what you want (e.g., “Write a 100-word blog post”).
Details: Add specifics (e.g., “For a local coffee shop targeting young professionals”).
Tone/Style: Set the vibe (e.g., “Friendly and engaging”).
Constraints: Limit scope (e.g., “Use simple language, no jargon”).
Example Prompt: “Act as a web designer. Create a landing page outline for a local bakery targeting families, with a warm and inviting tone. Include a headline, subheadline, and call-to-action. Keep it under 200 words.”
Quick Activity: "Let’s try it! Turn to your neighbor and brainstorm a prompt for your business in 30 seconds. For example, ask Grok to draft a social media post or analyze your expenses.”
Engagement Tip: Share a quick success story: “A local restaurant used a simple prompt to generate a week’s worth of Instagram posts in 10 minutes, saving hours of work.”
Tools Mentioned: ChatGPT, Grok (free on x.com with limits) – empower SMBs by providing accessible platforms for content creation and problem-solving.
3. Why AI for Your Small Business? (8 minutes)
Goal: Highlight AI’s value in addressing SMB struggles like limited time, budget, and scale.
Talking Points:
Competitive Edge (2 min): "SMBs adopting AI make decisions 3x faster (PwC, 2023). Imagine outpacing competitors by quickly spotting trends or automating tasks."
Cost Savings (2 min): "AI can cut operational costs by 15-30% (McKinsey). For example, automating inventory tracking means less waste and fewer stockouts."
Increased Revenue (2 min): "Personalized marketing powered by AI can boost sales by 10-20%. Think targeted ads that hit the right customers every time.”
Enhanced Efficiency (2 min): "AI can automate 40% of repetitive tasks, like invoicing or scheduling, freeing you to focus on growth."
Relate to SMB Struggles: "As small business owners, Josh and I know the grind. AI helped MoilApp streamline hiring for offline workers, and JUCO AI uses it to personalize student support. You can do the same.”
Engagement Tip: Ask, “Which of these benefits—saving time, cutting costs, or boosting sales—would make the biggest difference for your business?”
Tools Mentioned: None explicitly, but the benefits set the stage for specific tools in later sections.

Goal: Show concrete AI tools for customer engagement and marketing, emphasizing the need to understand goals to avoid creating "noise."
Talking Points:
Understand Your Goals First (2 min): "Before using AI for marketing, clarify your goal—whether it’s more leads, brand awareness, or customer loyalty. Without a clear target, you risk creating noise that drowns out your message. For example, a local gym might focus on attracting new members with targeted ads rather than generic posts."
24/7 Customer Support (2 min): "AI chatbots, like those on JUCO AI, cut support costs by 30% and respond 50% faster. Imagine never losing a customer because you couldn’t answer at midnight.”
Personalized Marketing (2 min): "AI-driven email campaigns, like those from Mailchimp’s AI, get 20% higher click-through rates. Define your audience first, then let AI craft offers they’ll love.”
Content Creation (2 min): "Tools like Jasper AI or Grok create blog posts or social media updates 80% faster—but align them with your goals. A bakery could generate a month’s content in an afternoon, but only if it’s designed to drive sales or foot traffic.”
Sales Enablement (1 min, adjusted for time): "AI improves lead scoring by 15-25%, so you focus on customers most likely to buy, saving time and boosting ROI.”
SMB Focus: "For small teams, these tools mean you don’t need a marketing department to compete, but strategy comes first to cut through the clutter.”
Engagement Tip: Share a JUCO AI example: “We used AI to create personalized outreach for students, doubling engagement by focusing on their specific needs.”
Tools Mentioned:
Mailchimp AI: For personalized email campaigns, accessible for SMBs with limited marketing expertise.
Jasper AI: For fast content creation, ideal for SMBs needing blog posts or social media.
Grok: Free (with limits on x.com), versatile for content and customer support.
Chatbots (e.g., JUCO AI’s): Highlight their role in 24/7 support, empowering small teams.

6. Getting Started with AI: Actionable Steps (8 minutes)
Goal: Provide a clear, low-barrier roadmap for SMBs to adopt AI, emphasizing critical engagement to maintain identity and control over AI outputs.

Low-Cost &amp; Free Options (2 min): "Tools like ChatGPT, Grok (free on x.com with limits), or Canva’s Magic Studio are affordable starting points.”
Pilot Projects (1 min): "Test AI with a small budget—$500-$2,000 for initial software. Measure results in 30 days to see what works.”
Shape AI to Fit You (1 min): “Do not become what the AI gives you—make the AI become you. Don’t lose yourself. Question its outputs, have conversations, spark debate, dig deeper. For example, if Grok writes a blog post, tweak it to match your brand’s voice. AI is a tool, not your boss.”
SMB Focus: “You don’t need to be a tech expert. These tools are user-friendly and built for small businesses with tight budgets.”
Engagement Tip: “What’s one task you’d love to automate? Let’s brainstorm how AI can help after the demo.”
Tools Mentioned:

ChatGPT, Grok: Free/low-cost, versatile for various tasks.
Canva’s Magic Studio: For design tasks, empowering SMBs with no design team.
7. Live Demo: Creating a Landing Page with One Prompt (15 minutes)
Goal: Show the power of AI with a high-impact, live landing page creation using a single prompt.
Preparation:
Use a tool like Grok (available on x.com) or ChatGPT for the demo.
Pre-test the prompt to ensure it generates a clean landing page outline.
Have a laptop/projector ready to display the AI interface and output.
Talking Points (2 min setup):
“Let’s put AI to work! We’re going to create a professional landing page for a fictional small business—a local coffee shop—in just 15 minutes using one prompt. This shows how fast and powerful AI can be.”
“We’ll use Grok, which you can access for free on x.com. The same process works with ChatGPT or other tools.”
Demo Prompt (1 min):
Share the prompt on-screen:
“Act as a web designer. Create a landing page outline for a local coffee shop targeting young professionals. Include a headline, subheadline, call-to-action, and key sections like ‘About Us’ and ‘Menu.’ Use a modern, friendly tone. Keep it under 300 words and format as HTML for easy use.”
Explain: “This prompt follows our structure: role (web designer), task (landing page outline), details (coffee shop, young professionals), tone (modern, friendly), and constraints (under 300 words, HTML).”
Live Demo (10 min):
Input the prompt into Grok/ChatGPT live.
Show the output (e.g., HTML code or text outline).
Copy the HTML into a simple web editor (e.g., CodePen or a local HTML file) to display a basic rendered page.
Highlight key elements: “See how the headline grabs attention? The call-to-action invites customers to order online. This took minutes, not hours.”
Wrap-Up (2 min):
“You can do this for your business—tweak the prompt for your industry or audience. No coding skills needed!”
“This is just one example of how AI empowers small teams to create professional assets fast.”
Engagement Tip: Invite a volunteer to suggest a business type (e.g., bakery, gym) and tweak the prompt live for variety, if time allows.
Tools Mentioned:
Grok, ChatGPT: For the demo, showcasing their ease of use for non-technical SMBs.
CodePen (optional): For rendering HTML, accessible and free.

Key Tips for Delivery
Take Control Without an MC: Start confidently, set the agenda clearly, and transition smoothly between sections. Use phrases like, “Next, let’s dive into…” or “Josh, want to add your perspective?”
Empower the Audience: Frame AI as accessible and practical. Use relatable examples (e.g., local businesses) and avoid jargon.
Balance with Josh: Alternate speaking roles. For example, Andres leads “What is AI?” and “Operations”; Josh leads “Why AI?” and “Marketing.” Split Q&amp;A evenly.
Time Management: Stick to the 75-minute total (5 + 10 + 8 + 8 + 8 + 8 + 15 + 6 + 3 + 10). Use a timer or watch to stay on track.
Visuals: Reference slides (image1.png to image19.png) for each section. For the demo, show the AI interface live instead of a static slide.
Handouts: Provide a one-pager with key tools (Grok, ChatGPT, MoilApp links, Mailchimp AI, Jasper AI, QuickBooks AI, Canva’s Magic Studio, Microsoft 365 Copilot), the prompt structure, and contact info (hive@budaedc.com).
