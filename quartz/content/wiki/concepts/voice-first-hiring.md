# Voice-First Hiring

**Type:** Concept — product thesis
**Last updated:** 2026-04-12

## Summary

Voice-first hiring replaces text-based job applications with voice interactions — phone calls, WhatsApp voice notes, or conversational AI — as the primary intake channel for job candidates. It's Moil's core product thesis: for blue-collar workers, voice is more natural, accessible, and effective than filling out a form.

## The Problem with Forms

Traditional job applications assume candidates can:
- Type fluently on a smartphone
- Read and write in English (or formal Spanish)
- Navigate multi-step web forms on a small screen
- Have 20–30 uninterrupted minutes to complete an application

Blue-collar workers — especially those who are mobile-first, working in the field, or limited-English — fail these assumptions at every step. **The form is the filter.** By using forms, employers eliminate the workers they actually want to hire.

## Why Voice Works

- **Natural interaction**: Describing your work history by talking is how people actually communicate
- **Mobile-native**: A phone call or WhatsApp voice note requires zero app download, no keyboard
- **Language-flexible**: Voice handles code-switching and informal Spanish that forms can't
- **Accessible**: Works for workers with limited literacy, limited time, or limited data connections
- **Higher completion rates**: Conversational applications have 3–5x completion rates vs. long-form web applications (industry data)

## How Moil Implements It

1. Worker sees a job post (on Indeed, WhatsApp group, flyer QR code, etc.)
2. Taps a link → WhatsApp conversation opens (no app download)
3. Moil AI asks questions via voice note or text in preferred language (EN/ES)
4. Worker responds in voice or text — whatever is comfortable
5. AI transcribes, normalizes, scores
6. Employer sees ranked candidates with key info extracted — no resume required

## Moil's Metrics

- **Application completion rate**: 58% vs. 22% industry average (for blue-collar roles)
- **Bilingual reach**: 58% of candidates reached vs. 22% industry average
- **Time to first candidate**: < 24 hours from posting
