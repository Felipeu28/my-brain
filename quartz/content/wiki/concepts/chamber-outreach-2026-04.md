## Summary

On April 9–10, 2026, Andres ran Moil's **first at-scale outbound campaign** targeting chambers of commerce, EDCs (Economic Development Corporations), and CDCs (Community Development Corporations) across ~20 states. 63+ organizations were contacted across 4 email templates, using Buda EDC/HIVE as the proof-of-concept reference case.

This is the primary mechanism for replicating the Buda HIVE model nationally.

## Campaign Details

- **Volume:** 63+ organizations
- **Dates:** April 9–10, 2026
- **Target types:** Chambers of Commerce, EDCs, CDCs, community business programs
- **Reference case used:** [[wiki/orgs/buda-edc]] / [[wiki/concepts/buda-hive]] — "Buda EDC used Moil to power their HIVE cohort"
- **4 email templates:** Cold intro → value hook → case study → breakup

## Strategy

The campaign uses the **EDC replication experiment** from [[wiki/moil/gtm]]:
- Hypothesis: The Buda HIVE model (EDC buys licenses for cohort) works at other Texas EDCs and beyond
- Kill criterion: 3 EDC pitches with zero interest
- Decision date: Q4 2026

**Next phase (May 2026):** Scale to 30–40 cold emails/day. Refine templates based on April responses.

## Moil Relevance

If even 2–3 of these chambers adopt the HIVE model, Moil gets:
- Recurring B2G license revenue (same structure as Buda EDC)
- Built-in user cohorts (chamber members = Moil's ICP)
- Geographic distribution outside Texas
- Credibility to raise or pursue larger EDC contracts
