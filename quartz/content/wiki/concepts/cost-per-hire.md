# Cost Per Hire

**Type:** Concept — hiring metric
**Last updated:** 2026-04-12
**Related:** [[wiki/moil/metrics]], [[wiki/concepts/smart-hiring]], [[wiki/radar/workforce-retention]]

---

## Summary

Cost per hire (CPH) is the total cost a company incurs to fill one open position, including advertising, recruiter time, interview time, onboarding, and the indirect costs of vacancy. It's the primary ROI metric Moil uses to sell against incumbent solutions.

## Industry Benchmarks

| Sector | Industry Avg CPH | Moil CPH |
|--------|-----------------|----------|
| Blue-collar (general) | $2,400 | $850 |
| Construction | $3,000–$5,000 | ~$900 |
| Restaurant (BOH) | $2,200–$4,000 | ~$800 |
| Warehousing | $1,500–$2,800 | ~$850 |
| Landscaping | $1,200–$2,000 | ~$750 |

*Moil figures: internal data from Moil website / moil-documents-2026-04-09*

## What's Included in CPH

| Component | Typical %age of CPH |
|-----------|---------------------|
| Job board advertising spend | 15–25% |
| Recruiter / owner time | 30–40% |
| Interview time (all parties) | 10–15% |
| Background check / onboarding | 5–10% |
| Cost of vacancy (lost productivity) | 20–30% |

## Why Moil's CPH Is Lower

1. **Flat subscription** vs. per-application pricing: Indeed charges $8–$15/application; 20 applications per hire = $160–$300 just in ad spend, vs. Moil's $15–$75/mo for unlimited
2. **AI pre-screening**: Owner spends 20 minutes reviewing top 3 (not 3 hours reviewing 30)
3. **Higher completion rates**: More completed applications = better candidate pool per dollar spent
4. **Faster time-to-hire**: 11 days vs. 32 days industry average → lower vacancy cost

## How to Use This in Sales

- Calculate the customer's current CPH: ask what they pay for job boards + estimate time spent
- Show Moil's CPH against their current spend
- Frame as: "You're paying $2,400 per hire. Moil customers pay $850. What's your monthly hiring volume?"
