## What This Tracks

The fast-moving landscape of AI applied to recruiting: tools entering the market, regulatory scrutiny, employer adoption, and risks. Relevant to Moil's product positioning and fundraising narrative.

## Why It Matters for Moil

AI hiring is Moil's core differentiator. Understanding where the market is going — and what risks emerge — shapes product roadmap priorities.

## Key Developments (2025–2026)

- **AI resume screening at scale**: Most ATS vendors (Greenhouse, Lever, Workday) have added AI scoring. Adoption is concentrated in enterprise; SMB is still mostly manual.
- **EEOC guidance (2023–2024)**: EEOC issued guidance warning that AI hiring tools can perpetuate bias. Employers using AI screening must be able to demonstrate non-discrimination. This creates liability for vendors.
- **NYC Local Law 144**: New York City now requires bias audits for AI hiring tools used within city limits. Other cities watching.
- **Candidate AI use**: Candidates are now using AI to generate resumes and cover letters → filtering arms race. Voice-first applications (Moil) sidestep this.
- **WhatsApp / conversational hiring**: Startups in LatAm (like Hireez, Whataspp-native bots) gaining traction. Signal that voice/chat > forms.
- **LLM interview bots**: Vendors like HireVue offering AI video interviews. Adoption mixed; candidate pushback on surveillance perception.

## Open Questions for Moil
- Does Moil need a bias audit to sell to enterprise / regulated sectors?
- As ATS incumbents add AI, how does Moil's voice-first approach remain differentiated?
- NYC-style regulation spreading → proactive compliance as a moat?
