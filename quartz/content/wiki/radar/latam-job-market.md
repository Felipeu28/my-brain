## What This Tracks

The job market for Latin American workers in the US — including immigrant workforce dynamics, remittance flows, employment sectors, and technology adoption patterns. Also tracks LatAm-origin labor-tech startups.

## Why It Matters for Moil

Moil's worker supply base is disproportionately Hispanic/Latin American (first and second generation). Understanding this demographic's job-seeking behavior, economic pressures, and technology preferences shapes product and go-to-market decisions.

## Key Dynamics

- **Remittance economy**: Many workers send 10–30% of income to family abroad. Economic pressure → job stability preference → high motivation to retain good jobs
- **Informal job market**: Significant portion of blue-collar job-finding happens through word-of-mouth, WhatsApp groups, and in-person networks — not job boards
- **Document status complexity**: Mix of citizens, green card holders, DACA recipients, work visa holders. Employers need flexibility; workers need discretion
- **Education gap**: 40%+ of Hispanic workers lack a high school diploma (vs. 8% white workers) → forms and ATS systems create enormous friction
