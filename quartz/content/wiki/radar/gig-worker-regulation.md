# Gig Worker Regulation

## What This Tracks

Laws and regulatory changes affecting the classification, pay, and treatment of gig and hourly workers. Directly affects Moil's employer clients who rely on flexible workforces.

## Why It Matters for Moil

Moil serves employers of blue-collar workers — many of whom use a mix of W-2 and 1099 labor. Regulatory changes affect how employers hire and how workers want to be hired.

## Key Developments

### AB5 (California, 2020)
- Reclassified most gig workers as employees; triggered nationwide debate
- **Moil impact**: California is not Moil's current market, but this set the template for other states
- Texas is still favorable: no AB5 equivalent; employer-friendly classification rules

### NLRB Joint-Employer Rule (2023–2024)
- NLRB proposed rule making franchisors and staffing agencies jointly liable for worker treatment
- Stayed by courts as of 2025; legal uncertainty persists
- **Moil impact**: Staffing agency customers could face increased liability → pushes toward direct hire (Moil's model)

### DOL Independent Contractor Rule (2024)
- Biden DOL tightened the economic reality test for independent contractor classification
- More workers should be classified as employees under the new rule
- **Moil impact**: Warehouse and construction clients using 1099 labor may need to hire more W-2 → increases hiring volume

### Texas Landscape (2026)
- Texas legislature generally resists worker reclassification laws
- But federal rules still apply
- Buda/Austin corridor employers are mostly compliant — they're small enough to hire direct
