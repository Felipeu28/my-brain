## What This Tracks

Research, case studies, and practitioner data on what actually improves retention of blue-collar and hourly workers. High relevance to Moil's pitch that better hiring → better fit → better retention.

## Why It Matters for Moil

Moil's core promise isn't just "hire faster" — it's "hire the right person so you stop rehiring." The 91% 90-day retention stat on the Moil website needs to be defensible and extendable.

## Key Research Findings (2024–2025)

### Top Predictors of Blue-Collar Retention (SHRM / Gallup data)
1. **Manager relationship** — #1 predictor; workers quit managers, not jobs
2. **Schedule stability** — unpredictable scheduling is #1 driver of voluntary exits in hourly roles
3. **Pay competitiveness** — workers within 10% of market median retain significantly better
4. **Commute distance** — workers >25 min from worksite churn 30% faster
5. **Language match** — workers who receive instructions in their primary language have higher retention

### What Doesn't Work
- Signing bonuses without underlying job quality improvement → 60-day bump, then same churn
- Generic "culture" programs without manager training
- Digital onboarding apps for workers who don't use smartphones fluently

### Moil's Angle

Moil's AI matching already factors in:
- Proximity (commute distance filter)
- Language preference
- Schedule flexibility signals

The case that better matching → better retention → lower cost-per-hire (amortized) is a strong sales argument. Build out the data.
