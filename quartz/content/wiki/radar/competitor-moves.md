## What This Tracks

What the major players in the blue-collar and SMB hiring space are doing — product launches, partnerships, pricing moves, and market positioning — specifically as it affects Moil's competitive position.

## Competitor Map

| Company | Category | Primary Market | Threat to Moil |
|---------|----------|---------------|----------------|
| **Indeed** | Job board | All segments | High — dominant reach |
| **ZipRecruiter** | Job board / AI matching | SMB + enterprise | Medium — moving down-market |
| **Wonolo** | On-demand staffing | Warehousing, food service | Medium — same sectors |
| **Instawork** | On-demand staffing | Hospitality, warehouse | Medium — Spanish-speaker focus |
| **Bluecrew** | W-2 staffing | Logistics, manufacturing | Low-Medium — agency model |
| **Workstream** | Hiring software for hourly | QSR, retail | High — most similar to Moil |
| **Homebase** | Scheduling + HR for hourly | Restaurants, retail | Low — adjacent, not hiring-first |

## Key Moves (2025–2026)

### Indeed
- Launched Indeed Smart Sourcing (AI-powered passive candidate outreach)
- Expanded Sponsored Jobs pricing — SMBs now paying $8–$15 per application (up from $3–5)
- No Spanish-first investment visible

### ZipRecruiter
- Acquired Breakroom (UK employee review platform) — signaling culture/retention play
- Launching ZipRecruiter for Contractors (1099 segment)
- Still heavily English-first

### Wonolo
- Raised Series D (2023); focused on enterprise logistics customers
- Not serving SMB segment; moving up-market
- No voice/mobile-first product

### Instawork
- Expanded to construction and cleaning (new sectors — directly overlapping Moil ICP)
- Hispanic market investment: launched Spanish-language app (2024)
- **Risk**: Instawork is the most direct threat in the Spanish-speaking blue-collar segment

### Workstream
- Raised $60M Series B (2023)
- Focused on QSR (fast food) — McDonald's, Chipotle operators
- No voice-first; no Spanish-first; no Austin/Texas focus

## Moil's Defensible Position

- **Voice-first**: No competitor has a voice-first application flow. Moil's biggest moat.
- **Spanish-first**: Instawork is entering this space; urgency to establish market position
- **SMB pricing**: $15–$75/mo is dramatically cheaper than Indeed's $8–$15/application model
- **Austin/Texas focus**: Local market expertise and relationships that national platforms lack

## Watch List
- Instawork Spanish app quality and market expansion
- Whether Indeed launches a Spanish job board
- Workstream expanding beyond QSR into construction/cleaning
