## Context

They had already had their session with [[wiki/people/joshua-edmond|Josh]] last week and chose to take Andres' session together rather than splitting. Josh's coaching style is methodical / structural; Andres' is "just go" / risk-tolerant. Christine and Kat already self-identify the same split (Christine is the impulsive jumper, Kat is the cautious planner with 10 years of operating Unwrapped Uncorked Events).

## Key decisions

1. **Anchor the entire business plan on the Buda EDC Train Depot opportunity** (~300–400 sq ft + outdoor patio). Below-average rent, 1-yr initial lease, EDC-backed marketing tailwind, low downside (the EDC will release them if it fails). Lowest-risk path to learning if the concept works
2. **Skip the Roundabout location.** Build-out loan + multi-year commitment kills unit economics. Sarah (the bookstore-with-wine concept) was talked out of it for the same reason — confirmed by Andres
3. **De-emphasize kid-friendly positioning during prime hours.** Each kid in the room ≈ 1 fewer paying adult. Reserve kid-friendly programming for slow days only
4. **Treat [[wiki/orgs/water-to-wine|Water To Wine]] (Dane) and [[wiki/people/sarah-hive-cohort|Sarah]] as direct competitors** despite friendly framing. Anyone buying wine from Christine + Kat is a buyer not buying from them
5. **Differentiation pillars:** experience-driven small space + community/family-named custom-label wines (using Water To Wine's labeling service so they don't need to make wine themselves yet) + a Buda wine-tour book (printed or QR code)
6. **Lock the 5-year dream; ship the 6-month version.** Don't chase the perfect forever-space
7. **Decide rent budget before any business-loan conversation.** A loan adds permanent overhead from day one and shouldn't be the first lever
