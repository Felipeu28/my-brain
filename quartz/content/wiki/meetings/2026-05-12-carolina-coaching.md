## Context

Second 1:1 with [[wiki/people/carolina-caro|Caro]] in 12 days. Between Apr 30 and May 12 she'd surveyed ~6 women from her network and was **leaning toward killing the women-coworking idea** because the artisan-entrepreneur respondents (Kerry the candle-maker, a macramé maker) said they needed *workshop* space, not a desk, and one frugal respondent ("Mary," HIVE Cohort 4) said she gets free meeting space at the library. Andres reframed: she'd surveyed the **wrong ICP**.

## Key decisions

1. **Don't kill the idea — fix the ICP.** The survey data was disqualifying because Caro interviewed artisan-makers (need workshop space) and frugal solopreneurs (use the library for free). They were never her client.
2. **Reframe ICP** as **women who already have income** (paycheck or revenue) who want community + a place to get out of the house — not entrepreneurs looking for free workspace. Buda demographics support this read.
3. **Lead with "community + work," not wellness.** Women feel guilty paying for wellness as a discretionary expense; they will invest in business/work. Spa/sauna/cold plunge/nails reframe as "while you're already here working" perks, not the headline.
4. **The 7,500 sqft backyard is the social engine.** Interior (~1,200 sqft) becomes curated, high-value. Outdoor space carries events + content + visible activity.
5. **Indoor layout (1,100–1,200 sqft realistic).** Lounge/working ~350 sqft · 3 phone booths carved out of existing closets · café/coffee bar in a repurposed closet · 1 meeting room ~120 sqft · reception/scanner only (no desk) · content/photo corner outdoors, refreshed monthly.
6. **Repurpose the larger bedroom's bathroom into a cold plunge + sauna combo** (Costco-style zip sauna fits in-bath). Hallway bathroom stays as the public/shared restroom — solves the "people staring at me when I exit the shared bathroom" problem of having it inside the workspace.
7. **Pricing direction:** $50–$100/mo membership. At $100 × 25 members it already covers the property; 25–50 more on top is profit.
8. **Sell corporate / institutional memberships, not just individual ones.** Andres-suggested anchors:
   - [[wiki/orgs/buda-edc|Buda EDC]]: $100/mo so Jackie + Casey can come work from the space and stay in front of women entrepreneurs.
   - Subaru (or similar luxury/family dealership): $300/mo for 3 seats — puts the dealership in front of affluent women buyers and gives Caro anchor revenue.
9. **Use Moil to drive ICP research.** Caro signed up live during the meeting, got the 30-day free trial activated. Andres confided (do not share yet) that **Buda EDC will give HIVE participants a 1-year Moil license** — a precedent that matters for the per-cohort Moil-license revenue line.
10. **Register a new business entity** for the coworking concept — separate from Caro's real-estate business. Liability + tax cleanliness.

## Coaching points captured (for KB synthesis)

- **"Plan your questions before asking them."** A coworking survey that just asks *"would you pay for a coworking space?"* is unfair to the idea. The right question depends on which ICP you're testing.
- **"Mary makes sense because she wants to save money — she's not your client unless she sees community as the benefit."** Distinguishing feedback *quality* from feedback *fit*.
- **"Move fast, create fast, fail fast — but play it against your market research first."** Use Moil to pre-test ideas before spending money.
- **"How do I start tomorrow?" beats a 10-year plan never started.**
- **Early-adopter pricing:** *"first 20 people, undeniable package — after that everybody pays full price."*
