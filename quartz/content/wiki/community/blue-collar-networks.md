## Overview

Trade associations, workforce development boards, and community organizations that serve blue-collar workers — especially in construction, landscaping, cleaning, food service, and logistics. These are Moil's **worker-side community channels**: the places where Moil's supply base (workers) organizes, trains, and finds jobs.

## Distribution Strategy for Moil

- **Workforce boards** (TWC, Workforce Solutions) → B2G contracts; funded by DOL → stable revenue
- **Trade associations** (ABC, NALP) → employer member channel; reach 100s of SMBs at once
- **Advocacy orgs** (LULAC, UnidosUS) → worker trust and word-of-mouth; critical for supply side
- **Community events** — job fairs, community days, ESL classes → grassroots worker acquisition

## Why This Matters for Moil

- Moil's supply side (workers) doesn't come from LinkedIn — they come from community orgs and word-of-mouth
- Workforce boards are a regulated, funded channel that most labor-tech startups ignore
- Trade association partnerships (one deal → access to 200 member companies) unlock employer-side scale
