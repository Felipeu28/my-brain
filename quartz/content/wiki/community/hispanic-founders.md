# Hispanic / Latino Founder Networks

**Type:** Community / ecosystem
**Last updated:** 2026-04-12
**Related:** [[wiki/community/latitud]], [[wiki/radar/latam-job-market]], [[wiki/moil/positioning]]

---

## Overview

A growing ecosystem of communities, accelerators, and funds specifically supporting Hispanic and Latino founders in the US. Andres operates at the intersection of this community as both a **member** (LatAm founder, Latitud LF6) and a **builder** (Moil serves Spanish-speaking workers).

## Key Organizations

| Org | Type | Notes |
|-----|------|-------|
| **Latitud** | Accelerator / community | Andres is LF6 — see [[wiki/community/latitud]] |
| **HACU** | Higher ed consortium | Hispanic-Serving Institutions; workforce pipeline relevance |
| **US Hispanic Chamber of Commerce** | B2B / advocacy | SMB network; potential Moil partner channel |
| **Prospanica** | Professional network | Hispanic MBAs; mid-market HR contacts |
| **Techqueria** | Tech community | Latinx in tech; recruiting / talent side |
| **NewMajo** | Media + community | Hispanic tech media; brand visibility |
| **Chingona Ventures** | VC | Latina-focused fund; warm intro target |
| **Palladium Equity Partners** | PE | Largest Hispanic-owned PE fund; awareness play |

## Why This Matters for Moil

- **Fundraising**: Hispanic-focused funds (Chingona, Palladium) are natural Moil investors — aligned on mission and market
- **Distribution**: US Hispanic Chamber has 200+ local chambers → potential Moil partner channel for SMB reach
- **Credibility**: being visible in this community signals authenticity to workers and investors
- **Talent**: Techqueria is a recruiting channel for bilingual engineers and PMs

## Gaps / Opportunities

- Most Hispanic founder networks are coastal (Miami, LA, NYC) — Austin is underrepresented → positioning opportunity
- Very few B2B labor-tech startups are serving the Hispanic worker market with a founder who speaks the language
