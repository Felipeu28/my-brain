# Goose (Open Source AI Agent)

**Type:** concept (AI tool — open source)

## Summary
Goose is an open-source AI agent framework that runs locally. Notable because Jack Dorsey (ex-Twitter CEO) personally endorsed it in April 2026: "People are sleeping on how excellent Goose has become under the hood" — 471K views. GitHub: aaif-goose/goose. Positions itself as a privacy-first, locally-controlled agent alternative to Claude Managed Agents.

## Key Points
- **Source:** github.com/aaif-goose/goose
- **Endorsement:** @jack, Apr 2, 2026 — 471K views. Jack Dorsey's posts carry substantial signal weight
- **Positioning:** Local-first, open source, no cloud dependency
- **Capability claim:** "Excellent under the hood" — suggesting the interface/marketing undersells the actual technical capability

## Why Jack's Endorsement Matters
Jack Dorsey's context: founder of Twitter and Square, now deeply involved in Bitcoin/open source infrastructure (Block). When he endorses an open-source AI tool, he's signaling alignment with his "open infrastructure" ethos. The Goose endorsement is notable because it comes alongside his "From Hierarchy to Intelligence" Sequoia essay — he's thinking about AI-native org design AND endorsing open source agent tooling.

## Relationship to OpenClaw/Hermes Ecosystem
Goose sits in the same space as OpenClaw + Hermes — alternative agent runtimes to Claude Managed Agents. The key differentiators:
- Goose: open source, local-first, Jack-endorsed
- OpenClaw/Hermes: community-driven, Claude-Code-compatible, plugin ecosystem
- Claude Managed Agents: official Anthropic, cloud-hosted, enterprise-grade SLA
