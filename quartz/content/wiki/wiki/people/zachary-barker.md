**Context:** Zachary is evaluating/using an LMS platform (Absorb LMS) for his entrepreneur programs. Wyatt Hook is the Absorb LMS technical contact. Josh Edmond also attended.

**Key discussion points:**
- **Tampa Bay expansion:** Zachary let slip (not for public circulation): *"The city of Tampa Bay is looking at this — that's a pretty significant size city for us"* — evaluating the platform for a Tampa Bay entrepreneurship program
- **Enrollment key architecture:** Zachary designed a HubSpot-triggered system where entrepreneurs scoring at different levels receive different enrollment keys → different "academies" or program tracks

## What This Means for Moil

Zachary is running the type of EDC program that Moil aspires to replicate at scale. His HubSpot-enrollment-key model is a proven architecture for:
1. Auto-gating program access by entrepreneur readiness score
2. Running multiple program "academies" (tracks) within a single platform
3. Scaling across multiple cities

The **Tampa Bay** signal is significant: if EDC programs of this type are being evaluated by major cities, Moil's B2G distribution model (sell to EDCs → licenses flow to entrepreneurs) has confirmed market validation at scale.
