## Summary

Caro is in a "stand by" professional moment. Background: 12 years running a café in Mexico City (closed during COVID), then a yoga studio in Mérida that was a personal/spiritual win but a financial loss. She's a graphic designer by training, certified yoga instructor (1,000 hours), holds an online master's in holistic psychology. The yoga studio failure left her hesitant to re-enter entrepreneurship.

She and her husband own a house in Buda backing onto Meridian. They built out a backyard — added a fence, cowboy pool, sauna arriving, outdoor furniture coming — originally intending a **holistic spa** but the City of Buda permitting process killed the plan after a year of effort and significant cost. House currently runs as an Airbnb at a loss (~$2,500/mo target rent vs. actual underperformance, especially Jan–Feb).

The session pivoted the concept from "holistic spa" → **women-focused coworking space** ("girl cave" / "woman cave" / "mancave for women"). Differentiator: a refuge where women entrepreneurs are uninterruptible by family, can build community with other women builders, and can host the occasional event/market without paying venue fees elsewhere.

## Key decisions

1. **Drop the holistic spa concept.** Buda already has a yoga studio (longstanding), a new spa in Meridian, and a physical-therapy practice with spinal alignment equipment. Adding another wellness venue would be undifferentiated competition. Caro agreed unprompted.
2. **Pivot to women-focused coworking** ("woman cave") as the core product — coworking by day, occasional small events. The spa amenities (cold plunge, sauna, cowboy pool) become **add-on perks**, not the headline.
3. **Tiered membership pricing model** — example structure floated:
   - Basic coworking: $75–$100/mo
   - Mid tier with one event per month included: ~$250/mo (vs. paying separately for venue + catering elsewhere)
   - Annual prepay discount (e.g., $900/yr instead of $1,200) to capture upfront cash and survive slow months
4. **Replace the Airbnb income, not exceed it (initially).** Husband's financial constraint: cover the $2,500/mo rent equivalent. Math: 25 women × $100, or 50 women × $75 = $3,750/mo (covers rent + leaves $1,250/mo). Tight but viable.
5. **Outdoor space = expansion lane.** The backyard pergola becomes another usable workspace if enclosed with retractable walls/blinds + AC unit (Andres pointed to Progress in Austin as the reference). Sheds in the yard could be converted into 2–3 additional private coworking pods over time. Bathroom permitting was already a blocker for the standalone backyard rental — needs a workaround.
6. **Closets become phone booths.** During the live video tour Andres flagged the bedroom closets as natural call-booth conversions (low-cost, high-utility for coworking).
7. **Smaller bedroom = bookable conference room.** Reservable for client meetings; foldable wall-mounted tables to flex between conference and open layout.
