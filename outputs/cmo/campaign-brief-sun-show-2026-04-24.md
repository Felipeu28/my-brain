## Objective

Generate 3--5 warm leads at the event who convert to paid Moil subscriptions within 7 days. Secondary objective: deepen Andres's presence as the local AI-for-small-business guy in the Buda community. This is the active GTM experiment (see gtm.md) -- we need strong data from this appearance to decide whether the Sun Show becomes a recurring channel.

## Target Audience

The Sun Show draws Buda-area small business owners, aspiring entrepreneurs, and community-minded residents. The sub-segment that matters most:

- **Service-based business owners (1--20 employees)** -- HVAC, landscaping, cleaning, salons, restaurants, construction. They're hiring, they're doing their own marketing, and they showed up because they care about the local community.
- **Aspiring entrepreneurs** -- people with a business idea but no plan. The 21-question onboarding hits them hardest because they walk away with a real market research report.
- **Bilingual operators** -- Buda's demographics mean a meaningful chunk of attendees speak Spanish at home. Full EN/ES support is a differentiator no competitor offers at this price.

Anti-targets: tech workers, franchise managers, anyone already using HubSpot/Salesforce (they're not Moil's ICP).

## Key Message

**Lead with:** "Answer 21 questions. Get a market research report, a business plan, and a 30-day content calendar -- right now, for free."

**Supporting messages (use in conversation, not on signage):**
- "500+ Texas businesses already use Moil. Travis uses it for Zoiwell." (social proof from someone they know)
- "It costs $15 a month. That's less than your Spotify subscription."
- "English and Spanish -- voice or text. Try it in whichever language you think in."

**What NOT to say:** Don't lead with "AI." Don't say "replaces your team." Don't pitch features. Pitch the outcome: you walk away with something real.

## Demo Script (3--5 Minutes)

The demo screen is the centerpiece. Here is the flow:

1. **Hook (30 sec):** "Want to see something? Give me your business idea -- or your actual business -- and I'll show you what Moil builds from 21 questions." Pull them to the screen.

2. **Onboarding (2 min):** Walk through 5--7 of the 21 questions live. Use voice input if the environment allows, otherwise type. Use Spanish if the person is more comfortable in Spanish. Let them answer about their own business -- this is the wow moment because it's THEIR data.

3. **Output reveal (1 min):** Show the market research report generating. Scroll through competitors, target demographics, market size. Say: "This would cost you $5,000 from a consultant. You just got it in 2 minutes."

4. **Content360 tease (30 sec):** Show a sample Content360 calendar. "This is your next 30 days of social media posts, captions, and images. All based on what you just told me."

5. **Close (30 sec):** "Scan this QR code, finish the other questions at home, and everything we started here is waiting for you. No credit card. If you want, I'll follow up next week to walk you through the rest."

**Pro tip:** If it's slow, demo on a pre-loaded account (Rockstar test account or Travis's Zoiwell account with his permission). If someone is skeptical, hand them the laptop and let them drive.

## Follow-Up Workflow

Every lead collected at the event enters the Brain within 24 hours:

1. **Same night or next morning:** Andres adds each contact to Apollo via the MCP (name, email, phone, company if given, tagged "sun-show-apr-24").
2. **Day +1 (Apr 25):** Personal follow-up message (text or email, not a template blast). Reference the specific conversation: "Hey [Name], great meeting you at the Sun Show. You mentioned [their business/idea] -- here's what Moil put together from those first few questions..."
3. **Day +3 (Apr 27):** If no response, one more nudge: "Just checking in -- did you get a chance to finish the 21 questions? Happy to walk you through it over a quick call."
4. **Day +7 (May 1):** Final check. If still no response, mark as cold in Apollo. If they signed up but didn't convert, check their usage and send a targeted tip.
5. **Winners drawing:** If using the "one of you won a free year of Moil 360" gamification (proven from last Sun Show), announce the winner on Day +3 via email to all attendees. This re-engages the whole list.

**Travis leverage:** Ask Travis for a short testimonial quote or a 15-second video clip that Andres can use in follow-up messages. Travis is a paying customer AND the event organizer -- that's the strongest social proof possible for this audience.
