## Summary
A B2B GTM framework where each channel amplifies every other channel — not siloed parallel tracks but a reinforcing loop. Originated from @MichLieben's series on how they grew from $40K to $7M ARR. Two key posts from the bookmark period:
1. "The Three Layers of LinkedIn Revenue Most B2B Companies Never Measure" — how they got to $7M ARR
2. "How to Build a GTM System Where Every Channel Makes Every Other Channel Better" — $40K → growth via multi-channel flywheel

## The Three LinkedIn Revenue Layers (MichLieben)
1. **Direct revenue** — inbound from LinkedIn posts and DMs (visible, measured)
2. **Amplified revenue** — deals closed by other channels where LinkedIn built the initial trust and familiarity (rarely attributed correctly)
3. **Compounding authority** — cumulative perception that makes every other sales conversation warmer, shorter, and higher-converting (almost never measured)

Most B2B companies only measure layer 1 and under-invest in layers 2 and 3.

## The Multi-Channel Flywheel
- **Content → SEO → LinkedIn → Cold Email** — each reinforces the next
- LinkedIn content makes cold email warmer ("I've seen your posts")
- SEO makes LinkedIn followers credible ("they rank for the thing they claim to know")
- Cold email drives LinkedIn follows for future warming
- Each channel's marginal return goes up as the others grow

## Why GTM Systems Beat GTM Tactics
Single-channel tactics produce linear results. A system where channels compound produces exponential returns from the same effort investment — this is how small teams compete with large marketing budgets.
