## One-line pitch

Moil is the AI co-founder every small business deserves — market research, business plans, a 30-day content engine, smart hiring across 10+ platforms, and 24/7 coaching, all from 21 questions, starting at $15/month.

## Competitive alternatives (April Dunford framework)

What does the customer use today, before Moil?

- **Hire a business consultant** — $5,000–$15,000 per engagement, 2–4 weeks. The "proper" option that most SMBs can't afford.
- **Cobble together point tools** — ChatGPT for writing, Canva for images, Indeed for hiring, spreadsheets for planning. Works but fragmented, nothing talks to each other, no business context carried forward.
- **Do nothing / wing it** — Most common. Owner handles everything manually, burns out, growth stalls.

## Unique attributes

- **21-question onboarding** — voice or text, EN/ES — builds a complete business DNA in 5–10 minutes that powers everything downstream
- **All-in-one platform** — market research, biz plan, Content360, hiring, coaching, docs in one place. No tool-switching.
- **Bilingual from day one** — full EN/ES support across every feature, 58% more bilingual candidate reach than industry average
- **Smart Hiring to 10+ platforms** — one job post auto-distributed, AI candidate matching at 95% accuracy, 11-day avg time to hire
- **$15/mo entry point** — 99.7% cheaper than a consultant engagement

## Value (what those attributes enable)

- 21-question DNA → Customer gets a 20–30 page market research report, investor-ready biz plan, and content calendar without hiring anyone
- All-in-one → Owner saves 11+ hours/week by not context-switching between 5 different tools
- Bilingual → Opens the full Texas bilingual labor market — trades, services, hospitality
- Smart Hiring → $850 cost per hire vs $2,400 industry average. 91% retention at 90 days.
- $15/mo → Removes the "I can't afford it" objection entirely for any operating business

## Customers who care most

- **Segment:** Service-based SMBs in Texas — HVAC, plumbing, cleaning, landscaping, restaurants, salons
- **Why this segment cares more:** They're hiring constantly (high turnover trades), need bilingual reach (Texas demographics), can't afford agencies, and their competition is still doing everything manually. Moil's wedge hits all four pain points simultaneously.

## Market category

- **Stated category:** AI business growth platform for SMBs
- **Why this category:** "Growth platform" signals that Moil does more than one thing — it's not just hiring, not just marketing, not just planning. The "AI" qualifier positions against legacy tools. "SMB" scopes away from enterprise.
- **Alternatives we rejected:** "AI hiring platform" (too narrow — misses Content360 and biz plans), "AI consultant" (sounds like ChatGPT wrapper), "small business OS" (overused, vague)

## Messaging hierarchy

- **H1 (the lead):** The AI co-founder every small business deserves
- **H2 (the proof):** 500+ Texas businesses using Moil. $850 cost per hire vs $2,400 industry average.
- **H3 (the differentiator):** One platform, 21 questions, everything you need — in English and Spanish

## What we don't say

- "Replaces your team" — Andres's customers ARE the team. Moil augments, doesn't replace.
- "Enterprise-grade" — signals complexity and cost. Moil is deliberately simple.
- "AI-first" — customers don't care about the tech. They care about saving time and money.
