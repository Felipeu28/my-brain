## Summary

**Major strategic pivot meeting.** Andres declared Moil is transitioning from a job-matching platform to a **comprehensive AI assistant for job seekers AND SMBs**. The market has revealed what's needed. Introduced the concept of an AI Interview Tool as the next major feature.

---

## Key Decisions — Business Model Pivot

- **Pivot declared:** From pure job-matching to an "ecosystem" of AI tools serving job seekers AND businesses throughout their journey
- **New product vision:** Resume creation → digital presence → interview prep → job searching → business tools (registration, branding, hiring) — all under one roof
- Customers need to be retained between job searches — need tools they use daily
- "We can't continue to educate people in a one-on-one setting" — build tools to scale

---

## Key Decisions — AI Interview Tool

- **Format:** Voice-only interviews initially (faster processing, lower storage, less intimidating than video)
- **Tool serves both:** Candidates (practice + analytics feedback) AND businesses (pre-screen filter)
- **Workflow:** Business posts job → sends voice interview request to candidate → candidate records answers → business gets analytical summary
- **Analytics:** Keywords detected, strengths, red flags, confidence, clarity, overall match quality
- Save up to 3 interviews per job title (with replacement option)
- Video deferred for later (Jacob to research cost/feasibility)
- **Blind hiring** principle: businesses evaluate answers before seeing appearance

---

## Key Decisions — Business Pricing Model

- Tiered packaging:
  - **Starter:** business registration, branding, logo
  - **Mid:** hiring tools
  - **Unlimited:** everything
- Alternatively: à la carte (pay per tool)
- Asks users "What stage of business are you in?" (idea / in business / growing) at signup to tailor offerings

Adeleke suggested creating a **marketplace page** where users can browse and access all AI tools. Andres was receptive. Connected to tiered packaging.
