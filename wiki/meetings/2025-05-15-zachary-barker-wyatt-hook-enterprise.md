## Summary

Enterprise customer call with Zachary Barker (CEcD), Wyatt Hook (platform vendor), and Josh Edmond (Moil). Discussion focused on API integration options for scaling Moil's white-label portal deployment to EDC/city clients.

## Attendees

- **Zachary Barker, CEcD** — Economic development professional; existing Moil customer
- **Wyatt Hook** — Platform vendor (API/integration specialist)
- **Josh Edmond** — joshua@jucoai.com — Moil-side technical lead for this account

## Key Points

- **API integration pricing:** One-time fee of $4,250 USD for integration APIs (subject to change to subscription model in future)
- **Sandbox access:** Wyatt offered a free sandbox environment for testing before purchase — no production impact
- **3 hours consultation time** included with any integration purchase
- **Enrollment key workflow:** Single reusable key (not per-user) embedded in URL; HubSpot dynamic scoring triggers key delivery (if score >= 70, then deliver key)
- **Tampa Bay prospect:** Zachary disclosed (confidentially) that the city of Tampa Bay is evaluating the platform — significant scale deal. Needs sandbox to test "academy" configurations without breaking other city deployments
- **Current workflow:** Cities get a unique URL with embedded enrollment key. No separate key entry needed by end users

## Decisions

- Josh Edmond to evaluate whether API investment ($4,250) makes sense vs. current HubSpot webhook approach
- Zachary to get sandbox re-enabled (was previously removed) for Tampa Bay testing
- Wyatt to follow up on sandbox provisioning

## Moil Relevance

This call reveals Moil's enterprise distribution model: white-label portals sold to EDCs/cities, with enrollment keys managed through HubSpot. The Tampa Bay prospect represents a major scale-up opportunity. The API vs. HubSpot-webhook decision point shows the product is maturing past manual onboarding into programmatic enrollment.
