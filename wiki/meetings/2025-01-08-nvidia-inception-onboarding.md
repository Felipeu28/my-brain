## Key Points

1. **Accepted into Nvidia Inception:** Moil was approved for the Nvidia Inception startup program
2. **$25,000 in credits:** Received 25 credits for Nvidia Deep Learning Institute (DLI) paid courses, plus access to free courses
3. **AWS credits context:** Also running on AWS credits — "the amount of money that we're saving right now" by having credits across both platforms
4. **Training plan established:** Andres directed the team to start with free GenAI courses as a group activity
   - RAG agents with LLMs (free, ~8 hours)
   - Building RAG agents with LLMs (free, 3+ hours)
   - Gen AI and large language models training for developers
   - Foundational AI, deep learning basics
5. **Daily learning culture:** Andres wanted 10-15 minutes of team training daily, with anyone able to share videos they found valuable
6. **Team access:** Jacob set up accounts; Andres could add team members through developer.nvidia.com manage users
7. **Technical audio issues** throughout the call — Andres's computer audio was broken

## Decisions

- Start with free courses as a team before using the 25 paid credits
- Focus on GenAI and RAG topics first (aligned with what Moil is building)
- Daily 10-15 min team learning sessions

## Moil Relevance

The Nvidia Inception acceptance is a credibility milestone — it validates Moil as a recognized AI startup. The $25K in DLI credits represent a training resource the team should still have access to. The focus on RAG courses in January 2025 foreshadows the BPC architecture (which uses retrieval-augmented approaches) and the AI Foundry chatbot (RAG over product docs) that became the #1 AI priority by May 2025.
